# Self-Review Response Scoring Rubric

Score each self-review response on four dimensions. The weighted average produces an overall score.

## Structure (weight: 25%)

How well-organized is the response?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Clear narrative arc — context, action, result. Each paragraph advances the story. Reader never wonders "why is this here?" |
| **4** | Well-organized with logical flow. Minor tangents but overall coherent |
| **3** | Organized but reads as a list of things done. Paragraphs could be reordered without loss |
| **2** | Loosely organized. Jumps between topics. Hard to follow the thread |
| **1** | Stream of consciousness. No clear organization. Reader has to work to extract meaning |

## Evidence (weight: 30%)

Are claims backed by specific, verifiable facts?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Specific, verifiable outcomes. References concrete deliverables, dates, metrics, and measurable results. Someone else could confirm these claims |
| **4** | Mostly specific. A few general claims but the core assertions are backed |
| **3** | Mix of specific and general. Claims like "improved quality" appear alongside real metrics |
| **2** | Mostly general claims. Few verifiable specifics. "I contributed to..." without saying what |
| **1** | Entirely subjective. No dates, no metrics, no verifiable claims. "I think I did well" |

## Outcome Focus (weight: 25%)

Does the response describe impact or just activity?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Every sentence speaks to impact — what changed because of the work. The reader understands *why it mattered* |
| **4** | Primarily outcome-focused. Activity mentioned only to provide context for the result |
| **3** | Mix of activity ("I did X") and outcomes ("which resulted in Y"). About 50/50 |
| **2** | Primarily activity-focused. Outcomes mentioned as afterthoughts. "I did X. It went well" |
| **1** | Pure activity log. What you did, not what it achieved. Could be a task list |

## Self-Awareness (weight: 20%)

Does the response show honest reflection and growth?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Honest about trade-offs and mistakes. Explains what was learned and how behavior changed. Shows the growth arc, not just the highlight reel |
| **4** | Acknowledges challenges meaningfully. Shows learning from at least one situation |
| **3** | Mostly positive, brief nod to challenges. "It was hard but I got through it" |
| **2** | Overwhelmingly positive. Challenges mentioned only to show how great you were at handling them |
| **1** | Everything was perfect. No growth areas, no trade-offs, no learning moments. Reads as defensive |

## Overall Score Interpretation

Calculate: `(Structure * 0.25) + (Evidence * 0.30) + (Outcome * 0.25) + (Self-Awareness * 0.20)`

| Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| **4.0 – 5.0** | Ready to submit | Final proofread only |
| **3.5 – 3.9** | Strong draft | Targeted polish on lowest-scoring dimension |
| **3.0 – 3.4** | Solid foundation | Rework 1-2 dimensions, add evidence |
| **2.0 – 2.9** | Needs significant rework | Likely too activity-focused or lacks evidence. Reframe around outcomes |
| **Below 2.0** | Start over | Fundamental reframe needed. Focus on "what changed because of my work" |

## Scoring Tips

- Score against the **level you're at**, not the level you want. A strong review shows you exceeded current expectations — that's the promotion argument
- **Evidence is the highest-weighted dimension** because vague claims are the most common failure mode in self-reviews
- A **4 in self-awareness** often matters more than a **5 in structure** — calibrators notice when someone is honest about growth areas
- When in doubt, read the response and ask: "Would someone who doesn't know me understand what I accomplished and why it mattered?" If not, the score is below 3
